Some definitions:

A quadrilateral is a plane figure bounded by four straight lines.

A square is a four-sided fiqure which has all its sides equal and all of its angles are right angles.

An oblong is a four sided fiqure which has all of its angles right angles, but not all of its sides equal.

Let

\(P\) = "the shape is a square" and
\(Q\) = "the shape has four sides of equal length"

Sufficient Conditions


Using the following Truth Table:


\( % line space \)

\( \begin{array}{|c c|c| c } % |c c|c| means that there are three columns in the table and % a vertical bar ’|’ will be printed on the left and right borders, % and between the second and the third columns. % The letter ’c’ means the value will be centered within the column, % letter ’l’, left-aligned, and ’r’, right-aligned. P & Q & P \implies Q\\ % Use & to separate the columns \hline % Put a horizontal line between the table header and the rest. T & T & T & ( i )\\ T & F & F & ( ii )\\ F & T & T & ( iii )\\ F & F & T & ( iv )\\ \end{array} \)


Case (i): If the shape is a square, then the shape has four sides of equal length.

The statement is True.

Case (ii): If the shape is a square, then the shape doesn't have four sides of equal length.

The statement is False.

Case (iii): If the shape is not a square, then the shape has four sides of equal length.

The statement is True.

Case (iv): If the shape is not a square, then "the shape does not have four sides of equal length".

The statement is False


The condition of P being true is enough (i.e. sufficient ) to make Q true; hence "\(P\) is a sufficient condition for \(Q\)."
