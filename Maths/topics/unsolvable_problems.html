--- title: Solvable and Unsolvable Problems layout: default --- In Alan Turing's article: Solvable and Unsolvable Problems he developes an argument that proves that certain types of problem are unsolvable. The Theorem to be proven is: The is no systematic proceedure by which one can tell whether a puzzle is solvable. Turing will use proof by Reducto Absurbumwhich attempts to establish a claim showing that the opposite scenario would lead to an absurity or contradiction.
