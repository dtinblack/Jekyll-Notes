--- title: Solvable and Unsolvable Problems layout: default ---

In Alan Turing's article: Solvable and Unsolvable Problems he developes an argument to show that that certain types of problem are unsolvable.

The Theorem to be proven is: The is no systematic proceedure by which one can tell whether a puzzle is solvable.

Turing will use proof by Reducto Absurbum which attempts to establish a claim showing that the opposite scenario would lead to an absurity or contradiction.

Let \(P(R,S)\) be the puzzle whose rules are described by the row of sympbols \(R\) and whose starting position is described by \(S\).
